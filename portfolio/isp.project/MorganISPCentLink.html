CenturyLink - Worth it?


About

CenturyLink, also known as Lumen Technologies is an American internet service provider. It was founded under the name Oak Ridge Telephone Company in the 1930s in Lousiana. It started as simply a telephone company, and over the years grew by absorbing smaller telephone companies. It changed its name to Century Telephone in the 1980s, but it was not until 2010 that Century began selling internet service as a part of its merger with Embarq. Now, CenturyLink is still headquartered in Lousisana and has become the fourth largest seller of both DSL and Fiber internet in the United States.


Types of Internet Avalible

DSL Fiber Copper
DSL internet, meaning Digital Subscriber Line internet uses exsisting phone lines and connections to provide access to consumers. This internet is not the most modern or the fastest, but it is relatively inexpensive and works well for most familes. Fiber internet, or Fiber Optic internet is some of the fastest internet avalible. It can travel up to 70% the speed of light, but this does come at quite a high price. Fiber Internet is far more expensive than average internet, but does show a significant difference in speed if that is a priority. Copper internet uses copper cables to send signals, this type of internet is one of the most common, and one of the most relaible. Copper internet is quite less expensive than types of internet such as fiber, and it is quite fast.
Within CenturyLink, DSL avaliblity in the Unites States is not the most widespread, (see image under limatations) Places like California and New York do not have access to CenturyLink. Even with limited access in specifc regions, CenturyLink is still the 4th most common DSL provider in the US. Fiber Internet provided by CenturyLink is limited to more populous areas. However CenturyLinks fiber internet market is the 4th largest in the United States. Copper internet is one of the main serivces provided by CenturyLink, as it is very standard for many people. Copper internet through this brand is still limited, but is avaible in over seven thousand zip codes.


Limitations of Century Link

The largest significant downfall of CenturyLink is its geographical limataions, it is only availble is thirty seven states. Large areas like California and the North East are unable to get CenturyLink internet. CenturyLink however is still able to be one of the most common and fastest ISPs avaible.
