About Red Pandas

Red pandas are bear-like animals about the size of a house cat, they are from the eastern Himalayan region and can be found mainly around Nepal, Bhutan, and China. These small herbivores mostly live in the trees and use their long fluffy tails for balance when climbing. The sad issue with Red Pandas is that they are extremely endangered.

Why Are They Endangered?

Red pandas are incredibly endangered, less than 10,000 of them are left in the wild. There main habitat of trees and bamboo is shrinking due to the area being cleared for things such as agriculture and deforestation. When Red Pandas are caught they are often trapped and killed or they are poached for their pelts. They are also threatened by other livestock and predators who encroach on their space. How You Can Help

  • Focus on sustainability, cutting down on trash and look into ways to reduce you carbon footprint. Climate change effects all environmental issues in this day and age.

  • Look into ecotourism when traveling, especially when going to areas where endangered or at risk animals might be.

  • Support organizations like the Smithsonian National Zoo and World Wildlife Fund that help endangered animals like the Red Panda.

  • Simply get the word out, spread information and help people become aware of these issues.
